Striving for Equal Opportunities for Children with Disabilities

All around the world, children with disabilites face unequal opportunites, including the opportunity to learn how to ride a two-wheeled bicycle.

How your community is working to support this cause Why you should give your support What you can do to help
Local organizations, such as the Easterseals iCan Bike Program, work to provide more opportunites for children. For example, their summer camp is designed to increase the balance and confidence kids have while riding a bike. Modified bicycles and trained volunteers assist kids throughout their journey of learning to cycle. Past research has estimated that 80% of people with Autism and about 90% of people with Down syndrome never get to have the experience of riding a bicycle. Your time and donations to local charities and organizations, including the Easterseals iCan Bike Program, as well as your efforts to spread awareness about this issue are critical to help fight the unequal opportunities children with disabilities have.
Transportation Exercise Improved mental health
Many kids with disabilites, as they grow older, might rely on an alternative means of transportation due to the safety risks of driving a car. Because of this, learning to ride a bicycle at a young age is critical for them to be able to travel independently. Cycling also provides children with physical benefits. The intensity and energy that cycling workouts require create a satisfactory cardiovascular workout to improve physical health. Lastly, cycling can improve the mental health of children. While cycling is often considered a social activity, its potential to decrease levels of stress and anxiety can improve the overall mood of cyclers.
